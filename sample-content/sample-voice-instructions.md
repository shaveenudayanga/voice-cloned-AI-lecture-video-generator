# Sample Voice — Why None Is Included

No sample voice recording is bundled with LectureVoice, and this is intentional.

**Voice recordings are biometric data.** A recording of someone speaking captures unique identifying characteristics of that person. Bundling a fake or synthesized "sample voice" would:

- Create a privacy/consent problem if the voice belongs to a real person.
- Mislead users about what the voice clone will sound like (a synthetic demo voice does not represent your voice).
- Potentially create licensing complications.

## What to do instead

Record your own voice directly in the app — it takes about 60 seconds.

1. In the LectureVoice wizard, click **Record your voice**.
2. Speak naturally for 60 seconds. Read from lecture notes, describe a concept, or just talk — any natural speech in your teaching voice works well.
3. The app will immediately play back a short test sentence in your synthesized voice so you can hear the result before committing.
4. If it doesn't sound right (background noise, echo, microphone too far away), click **Re-record** and try again.

## Tips for a good recording

- Use a headset microphone or sit close to a laptop microphone in a quiet room.
- Avoid rooms with hard surfaces that create echo (a carpeted office works well).
- Speak at your normal lecture pace — not slowly and not rushed.
- 60 seconds is enough; longer recordings do not improve quality.

## Your voice is saved for all future lectures

Once recorded, your voice profile is stored and reused automatically for every new lecture project. You will not need to record again unless you want a different voice profile (for example, a different language or a more formal tone).
